About the Professional Development Programme


The OECS Professional Development Programme is designed with the OECS educator in mind to support quality teaching, learning and leadership. As with the OECS PD Model, it keeps the teacher at the center of all professional development, with continuous professional development for all categories of educators in the OECS education system.

The programmeâ€™s approach is rooted in the convergence of findings from decades of professional development research. The first of its kind in the region, the programme offers choice to educators, emphasizing teacher-led and differentiated PD, and providing countless, varied opportunities for educators to develop the specific competencies required to meet the developmental needs of students. With implementation of the OECS PD Programme, the region has the potential to establish a new standard for PD both regionally and internationally.

Targeted Audience

Every Learner Succeeds


Education Officers

Education Officers encompass various roles, such as Permanent Secretaries, Chief Education Officers, Directors of Education, and Planning Officers. In some territories, Education Officers may oversee a group of schools or a district, while in others, they focus on supervising and assisting the implementation of a specific subject area, such as science.

Curriculum Officers

Curriculum Officers are those responsible for the quality of curriculum, assessment, and instruction. In some countries they are called Curriculum Officers or Education Officers (Curriculum). In other countries, the role is shared between Curriculum Officers and Assessment Officers.

School Leaders

In the programme, the term School Leaders include principals, vice-principals, and headteachers of Schools, Early Childhood Centers, and Special Education Schools and Centers. The Principal's main objective is to facilitate the teaching and learning process by supporting teachers and creating a conducive environment that enables them to operate effectively.

Teachers

Teachers are also considered those to be contributing to the development and learning of children whether they are assigned to mainstream, SEN or ECE schools. It is crucial that teachers provide the best experiences possible for learners to allow achieve their optimal performance.
