Professional Development Clusters

Ten (10) Development Clusters were created to support professional development across the region. There is a total of thirty (30) modules with brief descriptions provided below. These modules are based on the capacity building needs of the region.

Foundations of Quality Education

1 Modules in cluster

Foundations of Quality Education Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Foundations of Quality Education In this module, participants are introduced to the OECS Quality, Teaching, Learning and Leadership Framework, the OECS Curriculum and Assessment Framework and are introduced to the fundamental of the implementation of Change. The module demonstrates how the PEARL initiatives will support implementation of the Quality, Teaching, Learning and Leadership Framework and the Implementation of Change. Participants will explore:

  • The experiences of educators over the last two years and how to be best equipped for moving forward beyond the pandemic.
  • Restorative practices to ensure that they are able to function effectively with a myriad of responsibilities.
  • Evidence-based practices for quality teaching, learning, and leadership and how they can be advanced by school and system improvement planning.
  • The scope of change and associated risks, how to introduce change that can be sustained and how to identify and support those who are sponsors of the change identified.
  • Research and leading international practice about what constitutes meaningful practice that supports inclusion, authentic learning and includes classroom practices that emphasize constructivism, student centred learning, subject integration and increased discourse.
  • How equity, diversity, inclusion and accessibility are created within a school including multi-tiered systems of support where teachers and specialists work together to provide education that enables each learner to succeed.

(Consolidation of LEAD 1, LEAD 5, TEACH 1, TEACH 2, TEACH 12, LEARN 1, LEARN 5, LEARN 6)

Quality Teaching

3 Modules in cluster

Quality Teaching Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Holistic Instruction Participants will explore evidence-based teaching practices that promote academic, social-emotional and behavioural development consistent with the principles of the OECS Curriculum and Assessment Framework. Effective behaviour support and classroom strategies will be introduced as will lesson planning that provides learners with choice within time to explore and learn and time to demonstrate learning. The group will consider the meaning of “special needs” and establish a common nomenclature for use. How various diagnosed learning needs present themselves will be introduced as strategies for the inclusion of all learners discussed.

(Consolidation of LEAD 1, LEAD 5, TEACH 1, TEACH 2, TEACH 12, LEARN 1, LEARN 5, LEARN 6)
2 Meaningful Integration of Technology in Teaching Participants will explore digital literacy, digital citizenship and transformational practice incorporating technology to support enhanced learning outcomes. The use of technology to support Universal Design for Learning will be discussed. The concept of the blended classroom will be introduced along with the technologies that support teaching, assessment and learner engagement. Participants will prepare and present technology integration plans through an assessment of their current practice and an exploration of how technology might enhance their existing pedagogy.

(TEACH 7)
3 The Reflective Practitioner In this module, participants will:

  • An action research project that supports enhanced teaching and learning. This will include the setting of objectives, the design of the project that meets the objectives and the measures that will be used to assess the achievement of objectives.
  • The implementation of an Action Research and then implement the project designed through Lead 36. The implementation will be supported through ongoing coaching.
  • With coaching support, analyze Action Research Projects, assess the results and prepare action research reports.

(Consolidation of LEAD 36, LEAD 37 AND LEAD 38)

Quality Learning

2 Modules in cluster

Quality Learning Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Evidence-Based Practices for Quality Learning In this module, participants will develop lessons that demonstrate knowledge and understanding of constructivism and problem and project-based learning that support the acquisition and application of the knowledge, skills, values and competencies described in the OECS Curriculum and Assessment Framework. They will develop lessons that are designed to elicit quality discussions among learners that encourage and support the advancement of literacy. The will explore strategies for the gradual release of responsibility to learners using the principles cooperative learning strategies and their application in the classroom. They will consider the importance of context, particularly local context to the student learning and will consider how to link lessons to learners’ homes and communities in a manner that takes the learners from the known to the new. Participants will explore the meaningful engagement of the community in a manner that links their participation to curriculum learning outcomes.

(Consolidation of TEACH 5, TEACH 5, TEACH 8, TEACH 9, LEARN 10, LEARN 12)
2 Learning through the Creative Arts This module will celebrate the creative spirit of the Eastern Caribbean and how it can be captured as part of the learning in any of the core primary subjects. Participants will explore the learning opportunities that can be created through using Creative Arts as an integral component of teaching the core primary subjects and will develop lessons that incorporate the music, movement and dance, visual arts and drama of the region into language arts, mathematics, science and social studies.

(Consolidation of LEARN 11, LEARN 22, LEARN 23, LEARN 24, LEARN 25)

Child-Centered, Inclusive Practice

2 Modules in cluster

Child-Centered, Inclusive Practice Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Foundations of Child Centered, Inclusive Practice This module focuses on knowing your learners so that you are better able to support their education. Participants will explore effective methods of diagnostic assessment that provide background knowledge for tailoring their teaching and assessment. They will consider how they might use continuous assessment to chart each learner’s progress and work with colleagues to form learner-centered teams within the school where each student’s progress is supported in a meaningful way. Participants will create Learner Profiles that can be used to assess each student’s progress and will develop strategies for planning for the development of each learner. Teachers will learn how to determine when specialist support is necessary and how that support is provided. This will assist teachers with distinguishing between disengagement and true exceptionalities. Educators will consider how they might understand learners in a manner that helps create a classroom environment that is conducive to quality learning

Participants will explore how social and emotional conditions influence learning. This discussion will be extended to recognizing and responding to trauma. This module will also consider effective assessment and how to develop indicators that can provide the most valuable information about individual learner growth and achievement and well being. Participants will This use formative assessment to build on each student’s learning profile and chart their progress in a manner that provides meaningful feedback to the student and his/her family and serves as a guide to principals as they seek to support continuous school improvement. They will consider what information is needed to help them teach to the needs of each student in their class and will consider how to collect and share data will those who support the learners (teaching colleagues and parents/families). The OECS Grade 2 Assessments will be introduced so that participants can identify how the results might be used to support their teaching of individual learners who have completed them.

(Consolidation of LEARN 2, LEARN 3, LEARN 4, LEARN 7, LEARN 12, LEARN 14, LEAD 20, LEAD 23)
2 Transition Planning This module will assist school leaders and practitioners with key transitions that learners experience. Participants will develop strategies for supporting learner transitions:

  • From Early Childhood Education to Kindergarten;
  • From Grade 6 to Form 1;
  • From Mainstream Schools into SEN schools;
  • From SEN schools into Mainstream schools; and
  • Into Mainstream schools from schools in other jurisdictions

(Consolidation of LEARN 15, LEARN 16, LEARN 17 AND LEARN 18)

Quality Curriculum and Assessment

4 Modules in cluster

Quality Curriculum and Assessment Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Introduction to the OECS Harmonized Primary Curriculum In this module, participants will explore the foundations for the OECS Harmonized Primary Curriculum and the tenets which support it. They will discuss the OECS Curriculum and Assessment Framework and the foundational documents that informed it. They will be introduced to the concepts of Strands, Essential Learnings, Grade Level Expectations and Specific Curriculum Outcomes and how they contribute to continuity of purpose and establish a developmentally appropriate curriculum that is in keeping with the Curriculum and Assessment Framework. This will lead to an overview of the curriculum with the intention of preparing participants to work with the new OHPC.

(Consolidation of TEACH 10 AND TEACH 11)
2 Working with the OECS Harmonized Primary Curriculum During this module, participants will explore the various subjects in the new OHPC and will develop and share lesson plans using the new Language Arts, Mathematics, Science and Social Studies curricula. Feedback will be provided on the lesson plans. Through this process, participants will learn how to navigate the new OHPC and will develop confidence in its use.

(Consolidation of TEACH 15, TEACH 16, TEACH 17, TEACH 18, TEACH 19, TEACH 20, TEACH 21, TEACH 22)
3 Quality Teaching, Learning, Assessment and Inclusive Practices in the OHPC Participants will use the new OHPC to develop lesson plans that reflect the tenets of the OECS Curriculum and Assessment Framework. They will develop comfort in a new way of lesson planning Participants will be involved in using the OHPC to plan mini lessons designed to engage learners. The activity will utilize the accepted lesson plan template. Participants will discuss connecting the learning in one subject to the learning with a particular emphasis on how mathematics and science can be taught through language arts and social studies. They will develop lessons using anchor texts to support subject integration. Participants will explore the information that provides feedback on the achievement of learning outcomes and how this in turn, informs professional development and instruction.

(Consolidation of TEACH 3, TEACH 4, TEACH 13, TEACH 14)
4 Curriculum Evaluation and Design Participants will explore how a Curriculum and Assessment Framework is developed, including the consultation process necessary to ensure it reflects societal needs. The module will examine how the evolution of Curriculum and Assessment Frameworks that support sustainability occurs. Participants will review the process undertaken to critically evaluate curriculum using the Curriculum and Assessment Framework as the foundation and will explore effective curriculum design including translating a Curriculum and Assessment Framework into an operational curriculum and the development of Essential Learning Outcomes, Strands and Grade Level Guidelines based on the OECS Learning Standards.

(Consolidation of LEAD 39, LEAD 40, LEAD 41)

Quality Leadership

4 Modules in cluster

Quality Leadership Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Evidence-Based Leadership Practices In this module, participants will focus on how change might be effectively managed throughout an education system. This will include a further exploration of the identification of change champions and influencers and supporting their leadership of change. It will also assist participants to identify change resistors and detractors and develop strategies to respond to their behaviours. The session will explore techniques to manage resistance to change. They will learn how to choose priorities and create a system of education that is aligned with those priorities. They will explore the literature on crisis leadership and develop strategies to lead education systems during times of crisis. They will explore education management information and how it informs continuous system and school improvement and guides decision making at the school, district, national and regional levels.

(Consolidation of LEAD 4, LEAD 8 and LEAD 22)
2 Leadership of Change Participants will explore the building of implementation bridges that support change. Participants will reflect on the priorities for education that have been established and how they will facilitate them. They will then begin to construct implementation bridges to support change, identifying sponsor road maps, potential barriers to implementation and strategies to overcome these barriers so that change will happen as effectively as possible. Participants will explore how to develop effective communication strategies that support leadership and the implementation of continuous system improvement. The importance of assessing and qualifying senders and receivers will be explored as will the determination of meaningful messages for receivers. The session will emphasize the importance of sending messages that resonate with receivers while balancing the need for effectively communicating a message.

(Consolidation of LEAD 6, LEAD 7, LEAD 9)
3 Leadership of Curriculum Reform During this module, leaders will explore leading the introduction of the new OECS Harmonized Primary Curriculum. They will discuss working with their school teams to plan and deliver lessons using the new curriculum and how to provide effective and supportive supervision as teachers work with the OHPC. They will review their roles in supporting the viability of the curriculum through ensuring maximum teaching and learning days. They will better understand their role with the planning and implementation of lessons. School leaders of the pilot schools will consider their respective roles during the pilot while school leaders in Early Childhood Centres and SEN schools will develop strategies for effective leadership in their respective schools which supports the learning of each child entrusted to the care of their Centres and Schools. This module will involve school leaders commencing as a single cohort and then breaking into groups where specific strategies are developed for the curriculum pilot, for those leading ECE Centres and for those leading SEN Schools.

(Consolidation of LEAD 10, LEAD 11, LEAD 33 and LEAD 34)
4 Digital Leadership and Change Management School Leaders will explore leading schools that promote the pedagogical use of technology and discuss strategies for encouraging teachers to embrace modern teaching practices the incorporate technology. They will consider the 21st Century classroom and the transformation of traditional curriculum/school systems to graduate students that have competencies - regardless of technology access with reference to leading pedagogy; curriculum; technology; and change. They will consider the importance of encouraging responsible digital citizenship among the learners in their schools. They will consider the challenges that they face with introducing technology into their schools and develop strategies for effective integration. They will examine the possibility of creating blended classrooms and the tools that are available for creating these environments. They will consider technologies that support the development of higher order thinking skills. They will develop technology integration plans for their schools which include plans for professional development, teacher assessment, community support and parent communication.

(LEAD 42)

Continuous School and System Improvement

3 Modules in cluster

Continuous School and System Improvement Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Introduction to Continuous School Improvement Participants will be introduced to the OECS Continuous School Improvement Model and will begin to work with the model that supports the development, implementation and monitoring of continuous school improvement. They will be introduced to Innovation Configuration Maps that articulate the type of practice that is envisioned for teaching and learning and will learn how to develop and implement continuous school improvement plans that to the envisioned practice. Participants will discuss structures and processes that are supported for implementation and reflect on existing planning and implementation processes and how they could be strengthened. They will consider the OECS Continuous School Improvement Model and how it responds to the need for strengthening that have been identified.

(Consolidation of LEAD 12, LEAD 13, LEAD 14)
2 Evidence-Based Practices in Continuous School Improvement Facilitators will provide an overview of the continuous school improvement cycle which will be used in the pilot of the OECS Continuous School Improvement Model. Participants will explore the common data that schools use to assess progress with their continuous school improvement plans and consider how this data can then be rolled up to provide an assessment of the overall effectiveness of education at the national and regional level.

(Consolidation of LEAD 19 and LEAD 21)
3 Putting Continuous School Improvement Into Practice: Processes and Procedures In this module, educators will explore the prioritization, Alignment and Coherence in Continuous School Improvement and the respective roles and responsibilities of those who participate in the Continuous School Improvement process. Participants will commence the development of draft Continuous School Improvement Plans. They will share their continuous school improvement plans with their peers and discuss the data that was used to create the goals and describe any challenges that they encountered. An implementation processes that includes the creation of implementation bridges will be explores and strategies for facilitating team communication and collaboration that enable successful implementation will be provided.

A proposed template for conducting progress checks will be provided. Participants will develop specific implementation strategies for their own settings and will discuss how job-embedded professional development will be incorporated. Participants will participate in observation and coaching sessions to hone their skills in supportive supervision. The process will be debriefed to instill confidence among the participants in this process

(Consolidation of LEAD 3, LEAD 15, LEAD 16, LEAD 17, LEAD 18 and LEAD 35)

Early Childhood Education

4 Modules in cluster

Early Childhood Education Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Child Development: Creating Reasonable Expectations for Children Ages 3 – 4 In most jurisdictions, Early Childhood Centres are the first opportunity that parents have to determine the rate at which their children are developing. The incorporation of developmental assessment tools will be explored so that teachers in ECE Centres will be better equipped to assess where students are. Developmental checklists and scales will be introduced. Participants will explore child development and arise at a consensus of what level of learning is possible for young children. Models of effective programming will be considered. Examples from the region will be shared to demonstrate existing best practice.

(Consolidation of LEARN 20, LEARN 21 and TEACH 23)
2 Supporting Physical and Emotional Well-Being Facilitators will outline the needs of young children, how they express their needs and how to respond in a manner that supports their development.

(TEACH 24)
3 Learning Through Play Participants will explore the current research on play based learning and will discuss strategies that could be incorporated into their practice.

(TEACH 25)
4 Now that I know What I Want to Do, What Do I Need In My Classroom? This module will focus on the current research on the design and configuration of early childhood classrooms. This includes equipment and materials that support the development of all students.

(TEACH 26)

Special Education Needs

4 Modules in cluster

Special Education Needs Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Evidence-Based Practices in Special Education and Inclusive Education This module will explore evidence-based practices in individualized student assessment and instruction for students with special educational needs.

(TEACH 28)
2 Collaborative Team Approaches to the Education of Students with SEN This module will explore the use of collaborative teams of special educators and general educators in the education of students with special educational needs in various settings.

(TEACH 29)
3 Inclusive Practice for Students with Exceptionalities Participants will explore how teachers work with students who have exceptionalities in general classroom settings. The concept of Learning Centres will be introduced and participants will explore workstation purpose built construction and will discuss how assessment is incorporated into the use of Learning Centres. They will explore strategies for understanding student behaviour with the hope of ensuring that the knowledge will better equip teachers to respond. The session will focus on strategies for setting behaviour expectations and managing behaviours within the education setting. A portion of this module will be dedicated to preparing students for meaningful transition to the world of work. Participants will explore best practices based on research and experience of educators in the Member States.

4 Assistive Technology During this session, facilitators will review the various forms of assistive technology that is available. Teachers will share their experiences with assistive technology and the challenges of associated with ensuring that it remains continually available to students.

Assessment

3 Modules in cluster

Assessment Overview
# Module Name Module Description
1 Development of Fair and Equitable Assessments During this module, Assessment Officers will consider the importance of a well-written assessment to providing useful information which informs teaching and learning. As assessment questions are not always questions, they are referred to as “items”, this session will explore the development of items that result in fair and equitable assessment. Participants will explore how Tables of Specifications are developed to ensure that assessment is tied to curriculum outcomes and will develop Tables of Specifications for the OECS Grade 2 Assessments. The concept of performance tasks and how they have been used internationally will be discussed. An emphasis will be placed on the rationale for their inclusion in national assessments. The concept of portfolios to assess writing progress and how they have been used internationally will be discussed. An emphasis will be placed on the rationale for their inclusion in national assessments.

(Consolidation of LEAD 24, LEAD 25, LEAD 27 and LEAD 28)
2 Administration of Effective Assessments Assessment Officers will consider effective practices for the administration of assessments to ensure informative results. This session will consider the development and administration of sample questions, the professional development of teachers and the process that should be put in place within Ministries to ensure that assessments are carried out effectively. They consider how scoring guides are developed and used to score Language Arts and Mathematics Assessments in a manner that elicits the most information about student learning. They will then, with the guidance of the facilitator, develop scoring guides for the OECS Grade 2 Assessments that are designed to elicit the most information about student learning.

(Consolidation of LEAD 26, LEAD 29 and LEAD 30)
3 Collecting, Analyzing, Reporting and Guiding the Use of Assessment Data This module will examine strategies for collecting, analyzing, and reporting diagnostic, formative, and summative assessment data for various purposes, including classroom instruction and continuous school and system improvement planning. Participants will explore the tremendous role that regional and national assessments can plan in guiding priorities for teaching and instruction at the school level.

(Consolidation of LEAD 31 and LEAD 32)
