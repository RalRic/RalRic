More about the OECS PD Model and Projects

This section is dedicated to providing further information about the OECS Professional Development Model and identify projects that adopted the model to provide various training to strengthen capacity in the OECS Member States (MSs).

Professional Development Model

The OECS Professional Development (PD) Model was developed in 2019 by a group of educators from across the OECS with support from the Education Development Management Unit of the OECS Commission. The model has been endorsed at the highest levels, including the Caribbean Union of Teachers, the Eastern Caribbean Joint Board of Teacher Education and the OECS Council of Ministers: Education.

This OECS PD Programme is the first large-scale application of the model. While the model puts the teacher at the centre of the training provided, the approaches equally apply to other educators such as Principals, Education and Curriculum Officers and Teacher Educators.

OECS PD Model:

  • Teacher-centred
  • Differentiated
  • Job-embedded
  • Cost-effective
  • Innovative
  • Sustainable

Our Work

OECS Programme for Educational Advancement and Relevant Learning (PEARL)

The OECS PEARL is a four-year programme consisting of four main components: (1) Enhanced quality and equity of access for Early Childhood Education (ECE); (2) Enhanced Curriculum and Assessment; (3) Strengthened Leadership and Management; and (4) Program Management, Institutional Strengthening, Monitoring and Evaluation. The programme was designed in collaboration with OECS Member States and is positioned to make a major impact on the basic education system of the OECS through transformative and innovative activities related to curriculum and teacher professional development, in particular, guided by robust and meaningful monitoring, evaluation, reporting and learning.

Digital Education Capacity-Strengthening Initiative (DECI)

The OECS Digital Education Capacity-Strengthening Initiative (DECI) is a project supported by UNICEF and USAID to improve the delivery of Education to Grades K-3 in the OECS. The project aims to deliver 1500 curriculum-aligned high-quality lessons/units in the core areas of English Language, Mathematics, Social Sciences and Natural Sciences, and to train both teachers and school leaders in strengthening their abilities to leverage technology in Education. DECI was officially launched on June 10, 2022.

The five participating Member States in DECI are Dominica, Grenada, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines
